Historically Low Fertility Rate in South Korea

The Republic of Korea has maintained its reputation of having one of the lowest birthrates in the world in recent years. Even though it is a controversial issue whether this population trend brings positive or negative results for South Korea, it is certain that this would cause dramatic changes in social, political and economic environments due to the population shrinkage. This low fertility trend has been maintained its momentum for more than 30 years, and the country has already been seeing some substantial socio-political and economic changes such as social welfare crisis for elderly with aging population pyramid. This project is presenting a snapshot of current population characteristics with some visualizations.
